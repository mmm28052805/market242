Acrylonitrile (ACN) is an organic compound which is a colorless volatile liquid and is produced by ammonia catalysis of propylene. It has application in the production of homopolymers such as polyacrylonitrile and numerous copolymers such as styrene acrylonitrile (SAN), acrylonitrile styrene acrylate (ASA), acrylonitrile butadiene styrene (ABS) and synthetic rubbers. ABS is a thermoplastic, which is derived from acrylonitrile and exhibits the property of providing substantial weight reduction for automobiles.
